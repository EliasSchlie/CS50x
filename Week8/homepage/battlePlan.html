Battle Plan

My Strategy to Help Solve Aging

To effectively tackle aging, I need a comprehensive background in both computer science and biology. My first step is to acquire a solid understanding of AI, machine learning, and core biological concepts:

  • Complete Foundational CS & AI Courses: CS50 (Harvard), Andrew Ng’s Machine Learning & AI for Medicine Specializations, Deep Learning in Life Sciences (MIT)
  • Deepen Knowledge in Biology: MIT’s Intro to Biology (7.00x), recommended reading on molecular biology, genetics, and the biology of aging
  • Stay Current with Longevity Research: Follow reputable sources like Lifespan.io, Open Longevity, and the Rejuvenation Roadmap

Once I have a strong foundation, the next step is to explore specific domains within AI-driven longevity research to find where I can be most impactful. This involves:

  • Identify Potential Niches: For example, using generative models for protein design or drug discovery, analyzing aging-associated pathways, or developing geroprotectors.
  • Take on a Meaningful Project: Work on a multi-month research project that applies AI to a specific aging problem (e.g., exploring diffusion models in drug discovery, studying GFlowNets for scientific discovery).
  • Continuous Evaluation: If the project yields promising results and I’m passionate about it, I’ll pursue it further. Otherwise, I’ll pivot, try a different angle, and refine my specialization.

With a clearer focus and hands-on project experience, I plan to dive deeper into the field by seeking out or creating real-world opportunities:

  • Join an Impactful Organization: I’ll look for startups, labs, or research institutes actively working on aging interventions and longevity tech.
  • Contribute to Open-Source and Collaborative Projects: Participate in communities like Open Longevity, sign up for fellowships (e.g., LongBio Fellowship), and share insights to accelerate breakthroughs in the field.
  • Expand Network & Collaborations: Connect with researchers, entrepreneurs, and experts at conferences, hackathons, and online forums to foster collaborative projects.
