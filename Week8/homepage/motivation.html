Motivation

Why I Want to Solve Aging

I genuinely believe it's possible to solve aging. There are people actively working on it, and I want to be a part of that effort. Over 110,000 people die every day. Many argue that aging is inevitable, but what if it isn’t? What if we can treat it like any other condition and make death from old age optional?

The main objections to solving aging often revolve around ethics, feasibility, and practical concerns:

  • "I don’t want to live forever!"
    Nobody will force you. Making death optional is about having the freedom to choose when and in what state you die.
  • "It’s unnatural."
    Modern medicine itself is often ‘unnatural.’ Yet, it saves countless lives, including children with cancer.
  • Overpopulation concerns
    This is extremely unlikely to be an insurmountable problem. Human innovation has repeatedly expanded our resource capacities.
  • "Only the rich will have access."
    The value of an aging cure or geroprotective treatments is so large that it’s unlikely governments and society would exclude the majority.
  • "I don't matter, nothing matters."
    If we embrace a perspective that nothing truly matters, then why not choose a life path that excites you? Working on longevity can mean more time to explore and understand this existence.

Ultimately, I want to see humanity transcend these biological limitations. I want to witness what is possible when our lifespans are extended indefinitely, allowing us to explore new frontiers in technology, knowledge, and even the far reaches of space.
